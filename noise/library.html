02 // SENSORY TRIGGERS

SIGHT (Visual Cortex Activation)

1. GOLDEN RATIO COMPOSITION
Place key elements at 1.618 intervals. The brain recognizes this pattern subconsciously as "correct" and beautiful. Creates instant trust.
2. FOVEAL TARGETING
Central 2¬∞ of vision has highest acuity. Place CTAs dead center. Surround with lower contrast elements to draw focus.
3. PERIPHERAL MOTION
Rods in peripheral vision detect motion. Subtle animations at screen edges trigger attention shift without conscious awareness.
4. DARK ADAPTATION
Dark backgrounds make pupils dilate. Dilated pupils = attraction state. Users literally become more attracted to your content.
5. COLOR TEMPERATURE
Gold (#d4af37) activates reward centers. Red increases heart rate. Blue builds trust. Use gold for CTAs, dark for authority.
6. Z-PATTERN READING
Western eyes scan Z-pattern. Place logo top-left, hook top-right, content middle, CTA bottom-right.
7. GESTALT CLOSURE
Incomplete shapes force brain to "complete" them. Use broken circles, partial text. Engagement through mental participation.
8. NEGATIVE SPACE
Empty space signals luxury and exclusivity. Crowded = cheap. Sparse = elite. Let your content breathe.
9. TYPOGRAPHIC HIERARCHY
Size contrast creates visual rhythm. 3 sizes max: headline (display), body (readable), accent (small/mono). Consistency = trust.
10. MICRO-CONTRAST
Subtle borders and shadows create depth without clutter. 1px lines, 5% opacity shadows. Professional, not flashy.

SOUND (Auditory Processing)

1. BINAURAL UNDERTONES
40Hz gamma waves enhance focus. Subtle background frequency creates heightened attention state without conscious awareness.
2. UI CLICK SOUNDS
Short, crisp clicks (5-10ms) create tactile feedback. Frequency 1000-2000Hz. Signals successful action, dopamine micro-hit.
3. SUCCESS CHIME
Rising pitch = positive. C-E-G chord progression. 300ms duration. Triggers reward pathway like video game level-up.
4. ERROR TONE
Descending minor second (E-Eb). 200ms. Creates discomfort without alarm. User wants to "fix" the error to stop the sound.
5. AMBIENT DRONE
Low frequency (80-120Hz) continuous tone. Creates tension, immersion. Think movie score suspense. Volume barely perceptible.
6. NOTIFICATION PING
Sharp attack, quick decay. 800Hz. Mimics phone notification = conditioned response. User immediately attends.
7. COUNTDOWN TICK
1-second interval clicks, increasing tempo near zero. Heart rate synchronizes. Creates biological urgency.
8. WHISPER EFFECT
Breathy, ASMR-style audio triggers intimacy response. Use for important reveals. "This is just between us."
9. STATIC BURST
White noise burst = transmission/radio feel. Creates mystery, "intercepted" aesthetic. 100-300ms.
10. HEARTBEAT RHYTHM
60-80 BPM low thud. Primal, visceral. Creates tension without explicit threat. Body synchronizes unconsciously.

DARK/HORROR SOUNDS (Goosebump Triggers)

11. INFRASOUND DREAD
18Hz - below conscious hearing but felt in chest. Used in horror films. Creates unexplainable dread and unease. Can cause chills.
12. TRITONE (DEVIL'S INTERVAL)
The "diabolus in musica" - banned in medieval church music. F to B interval. Creates instinctive discomfort, something is wrong.
13. PSYCHO STRINGS
High-pitched dissonant cluster. Mimics Hitchcock's shower scene. Activates fight-or-flight. Hair stands on end.
14. CREEPING DRONE
Slowly rising pitch with subtle wobble. Something is approaching. Used in every horror build-up. Unbearable tension.
15. REVERSE REVERB
Sound that builds INTO an attack instead of decaying away. Unnatural. Brain knows something is wrong. Pre-scare tension.
16. DISSONANT WHISPER
Multiple detuned frequencies creating "voices". Sounds like whispers at edge of hearing. Paranoia trigger.
17. METALLIC SCRAPE
Nails on chalkboard frequency (2000-4000Hz). Evolutionary danger signal. Mimics predator claws, breaking bones.
18. BREATHING IN THE DARK
Slow, ragged breathing. Something else is in the room. Primal fear of predator nearby. Immediate alertness.
19. THE STINGER
Sudden loud dissonant burst after silence. The jump scare sound. Massive adrenaline spike. Use once for maximum impact.
20. SHEPARD TONE (INFINITE DESCENT)
Auditory illusion of endlessly falling pitch. Used in Dunkirk. Creates perpetual dread, no resolution possible. Anxiety loop.
21. CHILD'S MUSIC BOX (DETUNED)
Innocent melody played slightly wrong. Corrupted innocence = maximum creep. Slowing down = dying/ghostly.
22. SUBHARMONIC PULSE
25Hz pulse beneath audible tone. Felt in organs. Creates nausea, chest pressure. The "brown note" territory.
23. DISTANT THUNDER
Low rumble with no clear source. Something massive is happening far away. Existential dread. Approaching doom.
24. THE VOID
Sudden complete silence after sound. Absence is scarier than presence. The moment before something happens.
25. BINAURAL ANXIETY
Beta waves (25-30Hz difference between ears). Induces alert, anxious state. Fight-or-flight preparation. Use headphones.

TOUCH (Haptic/Somatosensory)

1. VIBRATION FEEDBACK (Mobile)
10-50ms vibration on button press. Confirms action physically. Use navigator.vibrate([10]) on click events.
2. SCROLL RESISTANCE
Subtle CSS scroll-snap creates "weight" to scrolling. Content feels substantial, not disposable. scroll-snap-type: y proximity;
3. HOVER ELEVATION
Elements "lift" on hover (translateY + shadow). Simulates physical button depression inverse. Creates tactile illusion.
4. DRAG INERTIA
Draggable elements continue moving after release. Physics-based easing. Feels like real object manipulation.
5. TEXTURE PATTERNS
Subtle noise overlays create perceived texture. Brain interprets visual texture as tactile. Matte vs glossy feel.
6. PRESSURE SENSITIVITY
On supported devices, vary effect intensity based on touch pressure. Deeper press = stronger response. 3D Touch/Force Touch.
7. EDGE RESISTANCE
Elements can't be dragged past boundaries without "rubber band" resistance. Creates physical world rules.
8. TEMPERATURE ILLUSION
Warm colors (red/gold) feel "warm". Cool colors (blue) feel "cold". Interface can create thermal perception.
9. WEIGHT PERCEPTION
Larger elements with shadows feel "heavier". Affects perceived importance. Important = heavy = real.
10. DOUBLE-TAP CONFIRMATION
Require two taps for important actions. Second tap = physical commitment. Reduces accidental actions, increases intentionality.

SMELL (Olfactory Suggestion)

1. VISUAL SCENT TRIGGERS
Images of coffee, leather, smoke trigger olfactory memory. Show wisps of smoke, steam rising. Brain "smells" the image.
2. WORD ASSOCIATION
Words like "crisp", "fresh", "musty", "burning" activate smell cortex. Use in copy strategically.
3. COLOR-SCENT MAPPING
Brown = coffee/earth. Green = fresh/mint. Purple = lavender. Yellow = citrus. Colors trigger associated scents.
4. TEXTURE-SMELL CONNECTION
Rough textures = organic smells. Smooth = clean/sterile. Grain patterns suggest wood smell.
5. NOSTALGIC IMAGERY
Old photographs, vintage items trigger memory-associated smells. Grandmother's house, old books, rain on pavement.
6. FOOD IMAGERY
Even abstract food representation triggers salivation and smell memory. Use sparingly but powerfully.
7. ENVIRONMENTAL CUES
Forest imagery = pine. Ocean = salt/brine. City = exhaust/rain. Scene-setting activates corresponding smell memory.
8. RITUAL OBJECTS
Candles, incense, cigars, wine glasses. Objects associated with specific smells trigger olfactory imagination.
9. DECAY SUGGESTION
Rust, aged paper, oxidation patterns. Suggests age and organic decay smells. Creates gravitas, history.
10. CHEMICAL/STERILE
Clean lines, white space, laboratory aesthetic. Suggests antiseptic, clinical smell. Trust through cleanliness.

TASTE (Gustatory Suggestion)

1. SALIVATION TRIGGERS
Words: "crisp", "juicy", "sour", "sweet". Even non-food contexts trigger micro-salivation. Engagement through biology.
2. COLOR-TASTE SYNESTHESIA
Red = sweet/berry. Yellow = sour/citrus. Brown = bitter/coffee. Green = fresh/herbal. Universal associations.
3. TEXTURE WORDS
"Smooth", "crunchy", "velvety", "sharp". Texture words activate taste memory even in abstract contexts.
4. TEMPERATURE LANGUAGE
"Cool refreshment", "warm embrace", "icy precision". Temperature words trigger taste-related sensations.
5. UMAMI SUGGESTION
Words implying depth, richness, satisfaction. "Substantial", "deep", "satisfying". Fifth taste = completeness.
6. BITTER TRUTH
Associate important/difficult truths with bitter taste words. "Hard to swallow", "bitter pill". Makes message feel real.
7. SWEET REWARD
Rewards described with sweet language. "Sweet victory", "taste of success". Dopamine pathway activation.
8. METALLIC EDGE
Technical/dangerous content with metallic taste words. "Iron will", "steely resolve", "blood and metal".
9. EFFERVESCENCE
"Sparkling", "fizzing", "bubbling". Creates sense of energy, liveliness. Champagne = celebration.
10. AFTERTASTE METAPHOR
"Lingering", "stays with you", "leaves its mark". Creates sense of lasting impact. Memory formation cue.

03 // IMPLEMENTATION NOTES

USAGE IN LANDING.HTML
1. Copy desired animation CSS to landing.html <style> section

2. Add class to element: <div class="anim-pulse">

3. For sounds, copy the JavaScript audio functions

4. Combine multiple senses for maximum impact (visual + sound + haptic)

5. Less is more - max 2-3 animations per screen

6. Use sparingly at key moments: CTAs, reveals, confirmations
